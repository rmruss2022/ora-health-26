## Core Concept

A meditation & reflection app that makes daily practice inevitable through **community momentum** - not guilt, not streaks, just... people actually showing up together.

Think Open's "everyone gets prompted at once" vibe, but for meditation instead of oversharing your lunch.

## Core Features

### 1. **Join the Collective** (Main Flow)
**The Open Model:**
- App notifies everyone at the same time
- "500 people are meditating right now"
- Tap in, join the session
- See who else showed up (optional: friends, global)
- Post-session: share how it felt (1-word check-in? color? vibe?)

**Why it works:**
- FOMO that's actually healthy
- You're not meditating alone, you're part of a wave
- Removes the "should I?" decision fatigue

### 2. **Solo Mode** (Always Available)
- "Not feeling the crowd? Go your own way."
- Timer presets: 5 / 10 / 20 / 30 min
- Same visuals, no social pressure
- Option to share afterwards ("I meditated at 3am, who else was up?")

### 3. **Daily Reflection Prompt**
**The Secret Sauce:**
- One question per day, everyone gets the same one
- Examples:
  - "What are you carrying today that isn't yours?"
  - "Where did you feel most alive this week?"
  - "What would you do if you weren't afraid?"
- Share your answer or keep it private
- See community responses (anonymous or named)

**Why this matters:**
- Meditation isn't just sitting. It's processing.
- Open does this with photos. We do it with introspection.
- Makes reflection a **communal act**, not homework

### 4. **The Feed** (Community)
- Not Instagram. Not even close.
- Simple text posts: "Meditated 10 min. Felt scattered but showed up."

## What Open Does (For Reference)

Open is a social app that:
- Sends a notification at a random time each day
- You have 2 minutes to post a photo of what you're doing right now
- Everyone gets the same notification window
- Creates authentic "this is actually my life" moments
- No filters, no curation, just raw daily reality

**What we steal from this:**
- Synchronized prompts = collective energy
- Time pressure = authenticity (no overthinking)
- Community participation = habit formation
- Anti-performative = real connection

## Why This Isn't Hype / Why It Lasts

### What kills meditation apps:
- Guilt streaks (missed a day = feel like shit)
- Lonely practice (hard to stay motivated alone)
- Performative spirituality (Instagram monks)
- Feature bloat (do I need a chakra quiz? no.)

### What makes this sustainable:
- **Community momentum** - you show up because others are showing up
- **Low friction** - notification ‚Üí tap ‚Üí done
- **No judgment** - missed 5 days? next session is in 2 hours
- **Anti-performative** - no streaks, no leaderboards, just presence
- **Daily reflection** - the part that actually matters, built in

## Open Questions

   - Anonymous by default?

4. **Guided vs. Silent?**

   - Or optional ambient soundscapes (rain, jungle, silence)?

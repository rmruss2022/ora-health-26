## Question Order Rationale

**Flow Structure:**

1. **Opening (Q1-Q3):** Broad, easy-to-answer questions that build psychological safety
   - Q1: Goals (welcoming, multi-select)
   - Q2: Focus area (simple choice)
   - Q3: Preferences (personal but not vulnerable)

2. **Middle Depth (Q4-Q5):** Slightly more vulnerable questions after trust is established
   - Q4: Biggest challenge (open-ended, skippable)
   - Q5: Check-in frequency (practical, low stakes)

3. **Practical Setup (Q6-Q7):** Logistical and contextual questions
   - Q6: Time preference (easy)
   - Q7: Therapy background (potentially sensitive, has "prefer not to say")

4. **Quick Assessment (Q8-Q9):** Concrete metrics and preferences
   - Q8: Stress level (slider, visual)
   - Q9: Motivation style (engaging, choose your favorites)

5. **Closing (Q10):** Open-ended finale that gives users final voice
   - Q10: Anything else (completely optional, feels generous)

**Transition Copy Between Sections:**

*After Q3 ‚Üí Q4:*
"Great! Now let's get a bit more personal..."

*After Q5 ‚Üí Q6:*
"Almost there! Just a few more quick questions..."

*After Q8 ‚Üí Q9:*
"Last two questions‚Äîthis helps us support you better..."

## Technical Notes

- **Required vs. Optional:** Q1, Q2, Q3, Q5, Q6, Q7, Q8, Q9 are required. Q4 and Q10 are optional.
- **Validation:** Multi-select questions need "select at least 1" validation (except Q9 which is "1-3")
- **Character Counters:** Display remaining characters on Q4 and Q10
- **Progress Bar:** Visual progress indicator at top of each question
- **Navigation:** Back button available (answers saved), no forward skip on required questions
- **Mobile Optimization:** Single question per screen, swipe or tap to advance
- **Accessibility:** All form fields properly labeled, slider has keyboard navigation

## Copy Tone Guidelines

‚úÖ **Do:**
- Use "you" and "we" (conversational)
- Keep it warm and encouraging
- Use parentheticals for clarification (friendly asides)
- Include emoji sparingly (1-2 per screen max)
- Validate all experiences as legitimate

‚ùå **Don't:**
- Use clinical language ("symptoms," "diagnosis," "treatment")
- Make assumptions about user's mental health
- Create guilt ("You should..." "Most people...")
- Oversell the app's capabilities
- Use excessive emoji or informal slang
